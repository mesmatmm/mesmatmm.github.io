Who I Am

About Me

I am a PhD student in Information and Systems Engineering at Concordia University, Montreal, Canada. My doctoral research focuses on developing probabilistic models in Machine Learning, with applications spanning intelligent security systems and data-driven decision making.

My background bridges mathematics and computer science — with an MSc in Computational Sciences from Cairo University where I researched chaos-based 3D encryption and published in peer-reviewed journals. I bring together rigorous mathematical foundations and a deep interest in Cryptography, Blockchain, and Quantum Computing to build secure, intelligent systems.

AI-SecureNet IDS

Python PyTorch Transformers Network Security

ChaosEncrypt

An image encryption system using the novel 3D fractional-order chaotic map developed in our MSc thesis. Passes all NIST randomness tests and achieves near-perfect entropy of 7.999 bits/pixel.

Python MATLAB Chaos Theory Image Processing

SecureChain Health

A privacy-preserving blockchain platform for healthcare data management integrating homomorphic encryption. Enables computation on encrypted patient records without exposing sensitive information.

Solidity Ethereum Web3.js Homomorphic Enc.

Deep learning steganography system using adversarial GANs to hide information in images with imperceptible visual distortion. Outperforms classical LSB methods by 12dB in PSNR metrics.
